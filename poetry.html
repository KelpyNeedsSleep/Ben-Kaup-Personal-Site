All Provide

The Ocean, violent and vengeful,
can never meet the still serenity of a pond.
Like a slow sloping hill, ever envious of the grand and gallant Mountain.
But all have their merits,
The Ocean, seas of exploration and discovery for those in search.
A pond, connecting, teaching, inviting those to join the web of life.
The Mountain, ever taunting, always challenging those daring enough.
And finally, a gentle hill, constantly comforting, relaxing, and resting those ready.
All separate, all opposite, yet none lesser, all connected, all equal, all provide.

If We'd Never

If we never met,
How would our lives differ?
If we never loved,
How would my eyes shimmer?
If we never had to end,
How would I have grown?
If we never missed,
How would I have ever flown?

If we never kissed,
How would i now exist.

Fuscian

When all crumbles away,
what will be left to decay?
If no one is here to observe,
what is left to preserve?
When all is estrange,
how will the universe change?
As time takes hold,
all turns old.

To Fly

I envision hubris, as wax wings daring to embrace the sun.
I wonder, is it so wrong—to fly?
To be free?
To want more?
To trespass into the domain of the gods?
For if the gods made us in their image,
why may we not inhabit the same painting?

Why must the gods hate us so, for desires that they taught us, they entrusted to us, they dangled in front of us?

Is it so wrong to want to fall?
For if the gods can sear the skin of such a hapless boy,
for the simple sin of joining their feast,
then why don’t we join him—in the fall?
For Icarus, never alone in flight—
only the fall.

The Insanity, We Find In Vanity

Why is Narcissus hated so?
For his self-worship? self-lust? ego? hubris? pride in his own beauty?
Is it, his very beauty that condemns him so?
Or is it something deeper—his all consuming vanity. Self-love, that twists the soul, and warps the mind—
For what is the true difference between vanity and insanity?

Why may we not listen to the siren’s call of our own echo?
Where all float alongside the dancing notes, crashing into the awaiting rocks.
Why may we not explore the realm of our own radiance?
Where all bathe in the lustrous light, burning in the blazing sun.
Why may we not gaze into the pool of our own eyes?
Where all marvel at their own beauty, wasting away,
becoming but a flowery shadow, of a man, once blessed in beauty.

So this time I ask, what is the difference between vanity and insanity, if not one’s sense of self?
For in insanity you slip and lose grip on your mind,
while in vanity you grasp, clutching tight, until it suddenly suffocates— beneath love’s oppressive weight.
